This simple sourdough recipe is perfect for beginners, requiring only flour, water, salt, and time!

  • Ingredients: Flour, water, salt, sourdough starter.
  • Instructions: Mix, ferment, knead, proof, and bake.
  1. Make the dough: Mix 500g of bread flour and 350g of water in a large bowl, let rest for 30 minutes, then add 100g of starter and 10g of salt and mix well.
  2. Proofing: Cover the dough and let rise at room temperature for about 4 hours, stretching and folding the dough every 30 minutes for the first 2 hours.
  3. Shaping: Transfer the dough to a dough board, shape it into a circle, and let rest for 20-30 minutes. Then shape it into the final form and place it in the basket.
  4. Final proofing: Cover and proof at room temperature for 2-4 hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  5. Bake: Preheat the oven to 232°C, place the dough in the Dutch oven, cover and bake for 30 minutes, then remove the lid and bake for another 15-20 minutes.
  6. Cooling: Take the bread out and let it cool for 1 hour before slicing and enjoying.

Focaccia is a flavorful, soft bread topped with herbs. Perfect for intermediate bakers.

  • Ingredients: Flour, olive oil, herbs, yeast, salt, water.
  • Instructions: Mix, knead, let rise, top with herbs, and bake.
  1. Activate the yeast: Mix the yeast with 350g of warm water and leave for 5-10 minutes to activate and create bubbles.
  2. Make the dough: In a large bowl, mix 500g of flour, 10g of salt, and the activated yeast mixture. Add 60ml of olive oil and mix until smooth.
  3. First proofing: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and leave in a warm place for 1-2 hours to double in size.
  4. Shape: After proofing, transfer the dough to an oiled baking pan and press it evenly with your fingers.
  5. Final proofing: Drizzle olive oil, sprinkle with herbs and coarse salt, cover, and let proof for 30-60 minutes.
  6. Bake: Preheat the oven to 220°C and bake the focaccia for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
  7. Cool: Let the focaccia cool before slicing and serving.

Braided challah is a beautiful and delicious bread perfect for special occasions. Try adding a chocolate filling for a fun twist!

  • Ingredients: Flour, eggs, sugar, yeast, water, oil.
  • Instructions: Mix, knead, braid, proof, and bake.
  1. Activate the yeast: Mix 50g of sugar and 7g of yeast in 300g of warm water, and leave for 5-10 minutes to activate.
  2. Make the dough: In a large bowl, mix 500g of flour and 10g of salt. Add the activated yeast mixture, 2 eggs, and 60ml of oil, and mix until smooth.
  3. Knead the dough: Knead well for 8-10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  4. First fermentation: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.
  5. Braiding: Divide the dough into 3 parts, stretch each into long ropes, and braid them together.
  6. Final fermentation: Transfer the braided dough to a pan, cover, and let it ferment for 30-60 minutes.
  7. Egg glaze: Brush the braided dough with beaten egg and sprinkle sesame seeds if desired.
  8. Bake: Preheat the oven to 190°C and bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown.
  9. Cooling: Let the baked challah cool before slicing and serving.
